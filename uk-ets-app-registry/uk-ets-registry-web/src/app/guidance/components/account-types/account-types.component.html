Compliance accounts for participants in the UK ETS

Participants of the UK ETS that have been issued with a UK ETS permit or an emissions monitoring plan are required to hold an Operator Holding Account (OHA), or an Aircraft Operator Holding Account (AOHA) respectively.

These accounts are used for compliance but can also be used for holding and trading UK ETS allowances.

Compliance accounts can only hold UK ETS Allowances.

Your regulator will instruct the Registry Administrator to open an OHA for you once your permit has been issued, or an AOHA once your emissions monitoring plan has been issued. The Registry Administrator will then make contact with the Operators’ permit, or Aircraft Operators’ emissions monitoring plan contact and ask you to appoint a Primary Contact, and nominate the Authorised Representatives that will be assigned to the account and specify their permissions.

The nominated Authorised Representatives will then need to create a Registry sign in to obtain their User ID.

The Primary Contact will be required to arrange for the following documents to be completed and returned to the Registry Administrator by email:

  • Letter of Authority
  • Primary Contact Appointment and Declaration

The Registry Administrator will review these documents then submit a document upload request to each proposed Authorised Represented via the UK Registry.

If, once any requested documents have been uploaded and background checks have been carried out, the Registry Administrator considers you are fit and proper to participate in the UK Registry and approves your nominated Authorised Representatives, they will be validated in the Registry system and sent a UK Registry activation code.

Each Authorised Representative will then be required to log back into the system and enter the code. Once they have done this they will be enrolled and will have access to their compliance account.

The process to gain access to compliance accounts differs from the process described below. Please do not take any action until contacted by the Registry Administrator.

Trading Account in the UK ETS Registry

Trading Accounts are voluntary accounts used for trading in UK ETS allowances in the UK ETS Registry.

Before you can start to make an application for a trading account, 2 proposed Authorised Representatives must create a Registry sign in and obtain their User ID, as they will need this as part of the application process. If the nominated Authorised Representatives already have a new User ID for the UK Registry then they don’t need to create another sign in.

You will need to provide the following information during the account opening application:

  • details of the Account Holder – for a corporate entity this includes the registration number, if available
  • the name and work details of the Primary Contact
  • the account details, including account name and the billing address for annual subsistence invoices
  • User IDs of your nominated Authorised Representatives – at least 2 must be added at application stage, otherwise you will not be able to complete the application process
  • the permissions for each Authorised Representative

Once the application is submitted, the Registry Administrator will review the application and submit document upload requests to each proposed Authorised Represented via the UK Registry. These will be both personal and corporate entity documents.

If, once any requested documents have been uploaded and background checks have been carried out, the Registry Administrator considers you are fit and proper to participate in the UK Registry and approves your nominated Authorised Representatives, they will be validated in the UK Registry and sent a UK Registry activation code.

Each Authorised Representative will then be required to log back into the system and enter the code. Once they have done this they will be enrolled and will have access to the account.

Charges and fees

When making an application to the Registry Administrator or National Administrator you must also pay any applicable fee. Your application will not be processed until this has been received.
